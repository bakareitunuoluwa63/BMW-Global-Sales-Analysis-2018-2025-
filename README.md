# BMW-Global-Sales-Analysis-2018-2025-
Project Overview
This project analyzes BMW's global sales data from 2018 to 2025. The goal was to clean the dataset, explore sales patterns, and identify trends across regions and vehicle models using Excel.
The analysis focuses on foundational data analysis techniques such as data cleaning, pivot tables, aggregate functions, and data visualization.
Tools Used
Microsoft Excel
PivotTables
Excel Tables
Aggregate Functions
Charts and Data Visualization
Dataset Description
The dataset contains information about BMW’s global sales across different regions and models from 2018 to 2025.
Key fields in the dataset include:
•	Year
•	Region
•	Model
•	Revenue
•	Units Sold
•	Average Price
•	BEV Share (Battery Electric Vehicle share)
Data Cleaning Process
Before beginning the analysis, the dataset was cleaned and organized to improve accuracy.
The following steps were taken:
•	Trimmed text values to remove extra spaces
•	Checked the dataset for duplicate entries
•	Checked for missing values
•	Converted the dataset into an Excel table
•	Organized the data using Pivot Tables for easier analysis
Key Insights
Regional Performance
China recorded the highest overall revenue and also sold the most vehicles, with 6,256,750 units sold during the period covered in the dataset.
This suggests that China plays a major role in BMW’s global sales performance, both in terms of volume and total revenue.
Model Performance
The BMW iX was the most sold model in the dataset, with 3,136,912 units sold.
At the other end, the MINI recorded the lowest sales among the models analyzed, with 2,971,205 units sold globally.
Average Price per Unit by Region
To better understand purchasing patterns across regions, average price per vehicle was calculated.
The results showed:
•	United States – 63,882.95
•	Rest of the World – 63,879.42
•	China – 63,846.55
•	Europe – 63,809.33
Although China leads in total volume, the United States records the highest average revenue per vehicle. The differences between regions are relatively small, suggesting fairly consistent pricing across major markets.
Year-over-Year Sales Growth
Sales growth between years was calculated to understand how performance changed over time.
Growth results:
2019 – -0.19%
2020 – 10.03%
2021 – 0.00%
2022 – 1.54%
2023 – 3.06%
2024 – 3.64%
2025 – 2.11%
The most noticeable increase occurred in 2020, after which growth continued at a steady but moderate pace.
Electric Vehicle Trend
The dataset also includes BEV share, which tracks the proportion of electric vehicles sold.
The trend shows a gradual but steady increase over the years, indicating BMW’s steady shift toward electric vehicle adoption.
